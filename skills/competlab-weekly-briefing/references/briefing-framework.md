# CI Briefing Framework

> Reference for the competlab-weekly-briefing skill. Principles for writing effective competitive intelligence briefings.

---

## What Executives Actually Want From CI

Research across B2B SaaS companies shows that the most valued CI outputs share these traits:

1. **Brevity over completeness.** A 500-word briefing that highlights 3 changes beats a 5,000-word report covering everything. Executives scan, they don't read.

2. **Interpretation over data.** "Revenue grew 15%" is data. "Revenue grew 15% despite a price increase, suggesting strong demand — they have pricing power we don't" is intelligence.

3. **Actions over observations.** Every insight should point toward a decision. If it doesn't suggest an action, reconsider whether it belongs in the briefing.

4. **Pattern recognition over event reporting.** Individual events are noise. Patterns are signal. "Competitor launched a blog post" is an event. "Competitor has published weekly for 3 months and is now outranking us for 5 target keywords" is a pattern.

---

## The OODA Framework for CI Briefings

Structure your analysis using the OODA loop:

- **Observe:** What changed? (alerts, data shifts, new content)
- **Orient:** What does it mean? (connect to competitive strategy, market trends)
- **Decide:** What should we do? (specific, prioritized recommendations)
- **Act:** What's the first step? (make the action concrete enough to start Monday morning)

## Severity Calibration

Not every change deserves the same weight. Calibrate your language:

| Severity | Language | Examples |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Critical | "Requires immediate attention", "Act this week" | Competitor launched directly competing product, major pricing undercut, security breach |
| High | "Monitor closely", "Plan a response" | Pricing change, new feature category, positioning shift toward your space |
| Medium | "Worth noting", "Consider implications" | New blog content, tech stack change, minor trust signal update |
| Info | Mention in dimension snapshot, don't headline | Small content updates, DNS changes, minor UI tweaks |

## When Nothing Happens

Quiet weeks are the hardest to brief. Here are productive framings:

- "Stable landscape this week. This is an execution window — focus on [user's top priority]."
- "No competitive moves detected. Consider: are they building something we can't see yet?"
- "Your competitors published 0 content pieces this week. Every post you publish compounds your lead."
- "Market is quiet. Good time to invest in [dimension where user trails competitors]."

Never pad a quiet briefing with filler. Honest brevity > manufactured urgency.

## Cross-Dimensional Patterns to Watch For

These combinations usually signal something bigger:

| Pattern | What It Usually Means |
|---------|---------------------|
| Pricing change + Positioning change | Competitor is repositioning — likely moving upmarket or downmarket |
| Content spike + AI Visibility rise | Content push is working for AI discovery — learn from their approach |
| Tech stack change + New features | Competitor is rebuilding/modernizing — may be quiet now but preparing for a push |
| Trust signals added + Pricing increase | Building credibility before raising prices — classic enterprise play |
| Multiple competitors making similar moves | Market shift, not individual strategy — adapt or risk being left behind |
